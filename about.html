Background

A career built on community.

Amelia's career spans over two decades of direct practice across some of Australia's most diverse and demanding contexts.

She began her professional life as a youth worker in Melbourne, working alongside culturally and linguistically diverse communities. She then spent several years in the Kinglake Ranges following the Black Saturday bushfires, working in community development and recovery. The experience deepened her understanding of trauma, resilience, and what communities need to rebuild.

From there, Amelia worked with women recovering from domestic abuse, before going on to complete further study in holistic modalities and her double degree in Human Services and Social Work. More recently, she has completed Level 2 EMDR training, adding a highly effective evidence-based trauma therapy to her clinical practice.

Across all of it, her focus has remained consistent. She supports people who are navigating complexity, with care, skill, and genuine respect for their experience.

How Amelia works

What shapes her practice.

01

Trauma-informed

Trauma shapes how people think, feel, and move through the world. A trauma-informed approach means safety, trust, and your pace always come first.

02

Neurodivergent-affirming

All neurotypes are welcome. Sessions are adapted to your communication style, sensory needs, and strengths, not the other way around.

03

Strengths-based

Amelia works from a belief that people are the experts on their own lives. The role of the practitioner is to walk alongside, not to direct.

04

Social justice

Amelia holds a strong commitment to human rights, equity, and the social and structural factors that shape people's lives. Her work addresses complexity, not just symptoms.

05

Lived experience

Amelia brings her own experience of adversity and recovery into her practice. She understands what it means to navigate systems, to not be believed, and to find a way through.
