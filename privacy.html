1. About this policy

I'm Amelia Slater. I run Amelia Slater Community Wellbeing, a private social work practice based in Numurkah, Victoria, offering counselling, EMDR, and NDIS support via telehealth across Australia and in person locally.

This policy explains how I collect, use, store, and protect personal information about people who interact with my practice, whether as clients, referrers, prospective clients, or website visitors.

Confidentiality means restricting access to your information, and controlling how it's used or shared, to protect your privacy. This policy explains how that's done in practice.

The practice is bound by Commonwealth and Victorian legislation, including:

  • Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)
  • Health Records Act 2001 (Vic)
  • Privacy and Data Protection Act 2014 (Vic)
  • Health Services Act 1988 (Vic)
  • Family Violence Protection Act 2008 (Vic)
  • Children, Youth and Families Act 2005 (Vic)
  • Mental Health and Wellbeing Act 2022 (Vic) CHECK

As an Accredited Member of the Australian Association of Social Workers (AASW), I'm also bound by the AASW Code of Ethics CHECK and AASW Practice Standards.

Check with Amelia: AMHSW status (separate from accredited member, requires Medicare provider number) and any other professional bodies (Youth Workers Australia, EMDRAA, etc) to add here. Confirm Mental Health and Wellbeing Act 2022 reference (replaces 2014 Act).

2. What information I collect

The information I collect depends on what you're doing with my practice. In general:

For clients

  • Personal details (name, contact details, date of birth, address)
  • Relevant medical and mental health history
  • Information about your current situation, goals, and what brings you to therapy
  • Session notes, including content discussed, observations, and plans
  • Information about supports already in place (GP, psychiatrist, support coordinator, etc) where relevant to your care
  • Payment and billing information
  • For NDIS clients: NDIS Participant Number, plan management arrangements, plan manager details where applicable
  • For Medicare clients: Medicare card details, referral details, mental health treatment plan information CHECK

For referrers

  • Your name, contact details, organisation, and role
  • Information about the person you're referring (with their consent in most cases)

For website visitors

  • If you fill in a contact or referral form: only the information you provide
  • If you simply browse the site: standard server logs (IP address, browser type, pages visited) for security and analytics. See section 10 below.

3. How I collect it

I collect personal information directly from you wherever possible. That includes:

  • When you fill in a form on this website (contact form, referral form)
  • When you contact me by phone, email, or text
  • During intake and during sessions
  • From referral letters or notes from other practitioners (with appropriate consent)

Sometimes I'll collect information about you from someone else, like a referrer or a family member. When that happens, I'll let you know that I've received that information and what it contains, as soon as it's appropriate to do so.

4. Why I collect it

I collect personal information so I can:

  • Provide you with safe, effective, and well-informed therapy or social work support
  • Communicate with you about appointments, billing, and your care
  • Bill correctly through Medicare, NDIS, private health funds, or directly
  • Coordinate care with other practitioners involved in your support, with your consent
  • Meet my professional, legal, and regulatory obligations
  • Run a small private practice (recordkeeping, accounting, insurance)

5. How I store and protect it

I take the security of your information seriously. Records are stored using CHECK a secure, password-protected practice management system that meets Australian privacy and health record standards. Paper records, where used, are kept in a locked cabinet.

Specific protections in place include:

  • Strong passwords and multi-factor authentication on all systems
  • Encrypted storage and transmission
  • Regular backups
  • Up-to-date device security and antivirus software
  • Locked filing for any physical records
  • A clean desk/screen practice when working in shared or visible spaces
Check with Amelia: name the actual practice management software (Halaxy, Cliniko, Power Diary, Splose, etc) and confirm it's hosted in Australia or note where data is stored. Confirm specific security practices.

6. When I share information, and when I don't

What you share with me in sessions stays between us. I will not share your information with anyone else without your explicit consent, except in the following situations:

  • Risk of serious harm. If I have a reasonable belief that you or someone else is at risk of serious harm.
  • Mandatory reporting. If a child or young person is at risk, I have legal obligations under the Children, Youth and Families Act 2005 (Vic) to report this to the relevant authority.
  • Family violence information sharing. Where applicable under the Family Violence Protection Act 2008 (Vic) and the Family Violence Information Sharing Scheme, I may share information to assess or manage family violence risk.
  • Subpoena or court order. If a court or other legal authority requires me to release records.
  • Medicare or NDIS audit. If Services Australia or the NDIA requires access to records as part of compliance or audit processes.
  • Communication with referring GP. If you've been referred under a Mental Health Treatment Plan, I'm required to communicate with your GP about your care, as part of the Better Access initiative.
  • Clinical supervision. I discuss my work with a clinical supervisor as part of my professional development. Any discussion is de-identified.

If a situation comes up where I think information needs to be shared, I'll talk with you about it first wherever it's safe and possible to do so.

7. Telehealth and online sessions

Telehealth sessions are delivered via CHECK a secure video platform that complies with Australian privacy standards. The platform is end-to-end encrypted and is not recorded.

You're responsible for finding a private space for your session at your end. I'll be in a private space at mine. If a session is going to involve someone else (a parent, partner, support worker), we'll discuss that in advance.

I don't record sessions. If you'd like to take notes during a session, that's your choice, but please don't record video or audio of a session without discussing it with me first.

Check with Amelia: name the actual telehealth platform (Coviu, Halaxy Telehealth, Doxy.me, Power Diary, etc). Confirm encryption and recording status.

8. NDIS information handling

If you're an NDIS participant, I collect and use your NDIS information specifically to:

  • Confirm your eligibility for the supports I offer
  • Bill correctly to your plan or plan manager
  • Communicate with your support coordinator (with your consent) about your goals and progress
  • Provide reports for plan reviews where you've requested them

I work with self-managed and plan-managed NDIS participants. I'm not currently a registered NDIS provider, so I don't bill the NDIA directly.

I don't share NDIS-related information with anyone outside your support team without your consent, except in the situations listed in section 6.

9. Contact and referral forms on this site

When you fill in a form on this website, the information you provide is sent to me by email CHECK. I read it, respond to you, and then either:

  • If you become a client, the relevant information is added to your client file in my secure practice management system
  • If you don't proceed (you change your mind, or we agree we're not the right fit), the form data is deleted within CHECK 30 days

Form data is not stored on the website itself. The website is a static site (no database) and doesn't keep a record of form submissions.

Check with Amelia: what form processor will be used (Formspree, Netlify Forms, etc) and where data is stored before reaching Amelia. Confirm retention period for non-client enquiries.

10. Cookies and website data

This website uses minimal cookies. CHECK Specifically:

  • Functional storage: the website remembers your accessibility preferences (text size, font, background colour) on your device only. This information stays on your device and is not sent to me or anyone else.
  • Server logs: the web host (GitHub Pages) CHECK keeps standard server logs that include your IP address, browser type, and pages visited. I don't have access to these logs.
  • Analytics: the site does not currently use Google Analytics or similar tracking. CHECK If this changes, this policy will be updated.
Check with Amelia: confirm host (GitHub Pages now, Netlify likely later). Confirm no analytics being added. Confirm cookie/storage behaviour matches what's actually deployed.

11. Your right to access your information

You have the right to ask me what personal information I hold about you, and to request a copy. There's no charge for this for current or recent clients.

To request access, email me at amelia@ameliaslater.com or by post to PO Box 70, Numurkah VIC 3636. I'll respond within CHECK 30 days.

If something I hold about you is wrong or out of date, you have the right to ask me to correct it.

12. How long I keep records

I'm required to keep client records for CHECK at least 7 years after our last contact, or until 7 years after a client turns 25 if the client was a child. This is required under the AASW Code of Ethics, the Health Records Act 2001 (Vic) CHECK, and Medicare/NDIS rules where applicable.

Records are securely destroyed at the end of the retention period.

Check with Amelia: confirm retention period (varies by source, often 7 years for adults, longer for children). Confirm relevant Victorian legislation.

13. If something goes wrong (data breach)

If I become aware of a data breach that's likely to cause serious harm, I'll notify you and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) under the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme. You can read about your rights at oaic.gov.au/privacy/notifiable-data-breaches.

14. Concerns and complaints

If you have a concern about how I've handled your information, please raise it with me directly first. You can call, email, or write.

If you'd prefer to raise it externally, or if you've raised it with me and aren't satisfied, you can contact:

  • Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC): oaic.gov.au or 1300 363 992
  • Health Complaints Commissioner Victoria: hcc.vic.gov.au or 1300 582 113
  • AASW Ethics Complaints: for complaints specifically about my conduct as an AASW member, contact AASW directly at aasw.asn.au

15. Changes to this policy

I may update this policy from time to time. The date at the top of the page reflects the most recent update. If changes are significant, I'll notify current clients directly.
