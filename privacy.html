1. About this policy

Amelia Slater Community Wellbeing is a private social work practice based in Numurkah, Victoria, offering counselling, EMDR, and NDIS support via telehealth across Australia and in person locally.

This policy explains how Amelia Slater Community Wellbeing collects, uses, stores, and protects personal information about clients, referrers, prospective clients, and website visitors.

Confidentiality means restricting access to personal information, and controlling how it is used or shared, to protect the privacy of the individual.

The practice is bound by Commonwealth and Victorian legislation, including:

  • Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs)
  • Health Records Act 2001 (Vic)
  • Privacy and Data Protection Act 2014 (Vic)
  • Health Services Act 1988 (Vic)
  • Family Violence Protection Act 2008 (Vic)
  • Children, Youth and Families Act 2005 (Vic)
  • Mental Health and Wellbeing Act 2022 (Vic) CHECK

Amelia Slater is an Accredited Member of the Australian Association of Social Workers (AASW), and is bound by the AASW Code of Ethics CHECK and AASW Practice Standards.

CHECK — AMHSW status, Youth Workers Australia, EMDRAA, Mental Health and Wellbeing Act 2022 applicability to private practice, AASW Code of Ethics current version.

2. What information is collected

The information collected depends on the nature of the interaction with the practice. In general:

For clients

  • Personal details (name, contact details, date of birth, address)
  • Relevant medical and mental health history
  • Information about current situation, goals, and reasons for seeking therapy
  • Session notes, including content discussed, observations, and plans
  • Information about supports already in place (GP, psychiatrist, support coordinator, etc) where relevant to the client's care
  • Payment and billing information
  • For NDIS clients: NDIS Participant Number, plan management arrangements, plan manager details where applicable
  • For Medicare clients: Medicare card details, referral details, mental health treatment plan information CHECK

For referrers

  • Name, contact details, organisation, and role
  • Information about the person being referred (with their consent in most cases)

For website visitors

  • If a contact or referral form is submitted: only the information provided in the form
  • If the site is browsed without form submission: standard server logs (IP address, browser type, pages visited) for security and analytics. See section 10 below.

3. How information is collected

Personal information is collected directly from the individual concerned wherever possible. This includes:

  • Through forms submitted on this website (contact form, referral form)
  • Through phone, email, or text contact
  • During intake and during sessions
  • From referral letters or notes from other practitioners (with appropriate consent)

Where personal information is received from a third party (such as a referrer or family member), the individual concerned will be informed about what has been received and the nature of that information, as soon as it is appropriate to do so.

4. Why information is collected

Personal information is collected in order to:

  • Provide safe, effective, and well-informed therapy or social work support
  • Communicate with clients about appointments, billing, and care
  • Bill correctly through Medicare, NDIS, private health funds, or directly
  • Coordinate care with other practitioners involved in a client's support, with consent
  • Meet professional, legal, and regulatory obligations
  • Maintain practice operations (recordkeeping, accounting, insurance)

5. How information is stored and protected

Records are stored using CHECK a secure, password-protected practice management system that meets Australian privacy and health record standards. Paper records, where used, are kept in a locked cabinet.

Specific protections in place include:

  • Strong passwords and multi-factor authentication on all systems
  • Encrypted storage and transmission
  • Regular backups
  • Up-to-date device security and antivirus software
  • Locked filing for any physical records
  • Clean desk and screen practices when working in shared or visible spaces
CHECK — practice management software name, hosting location, security practices.

6. When information is shared

Personal information is treated as confidential and will not be shared with any third party without explicit consent, except in the following situations:

  • Risk of serious harm. Where there is reasonable belief that the client or another person is at risk of serious harm.
  • Mandatory reporting. Where a child or young person is at risk, the practice has legal obligations under the Children, Youth and Families Act 2005 (Vic) to report this to the relevant authority.
  • Family violence information sharing. Where applicable under the Family Violence Protection Act 2008 (Vic) and the Family Violence Information Sharing Scheme, information may be shared to assess or manage family violence risk.
  • Subpoena or court order. Where a court or other legal authority requires the release of records.
  • Medicare or NDIS audit. Where Services Australia or the NDIA requires access to records as part of compliance or audit processes.
  • Communication with referring GP. Where a client has been referred under a Mental Health Treatment Plan, communication with the referring GP is required as part of the Better Access initiative.
  • Clinical supervision. Practice work is discussed with a clinical supervisor as part of professional development. Any discussion is de-identified.

Where information may need to be shared, the client will be informed in advance wherever it is safe and possible to do so.

7. Telehealth and online sessions

Telehealth sessions are delivered via CHECK a secure video platform that complies with Australian privacy standards. The platform is end-to-end encrypted and is not recorded.

Clients are responsible for finding a private space for the session at their end. The practitioner will be in a private space at theirs. Where a session involves an additional party (a parent, partner, support worker), this will be discussed in advance.

Sessions are not recorded by the practice. Clients may take written notes during a session at their own discretion. Audio or video recording of a session by a client requires prior discussion with the practitioner.

CHECK — telehealth platform name, encryption status, recording status.

8. NDIS information handling

For NDIS participants, information is collected and used specifically to:

  • Confirm eligibility for the supports offered
  • Bill correctly to the participant's plan or plan manager
  • Communicate with the participant's support coordinator (with consent) about goals and progress
  • Provide reports for plan reviews where requested

The practice works with self-managed and plan-managed NDIS participants. The practice is not currently a registered NDIS provider and does not bill the NDIA directly.

NDIS-related information is not shared with anyone outside the participant's support team without consent, except in the situations listed in section 6.

9. Contact and referral forms on this site

When a form is submitted on this website, the information provided is sent by email CHECK to the practice. After review and response, the data is handled as follows:

  • If the enquirer becomes a client, the relevant information is added to their client file in the secure practice management system
  • If the enquirer does not proceed, the form data is deleted within CHECK 30 days

Form data is not stored on the website itself. The website is a static site (no database) and does not retain form submissions.

10. Cookies and website data

This website uses minimal cookies. CHECK Specifically:

  • Functional storage: the website remembers visitor accessibility preferences (text size, font, background colour) on the visitor's device only. This information stays on the device and is not transmitted.
  • Server logs: the web host (GitHub Pages) CHECK keeps standard server logs that include IP address, browser type, and pages visited. The practice does not have access to these logs.
  • Analytics: the site does not currently use Google Analytics or similar tracking. CHECK If this changes, this policy will be updated.

11. Right to access information

Individuals have the right to request access to personal information held about them, and to receive a copy. There is no charge for this for current or recent clients.

Access requests can be made by email to amelia@ameliaslater.com or by post to PO Box 70, Numurkah VIC 3636. The practice will respond within CHECK 30 days.

Individuals also have the right to request correction of personal information that is inaccurate or out of date.

12. How long records are kept

Client records are retained for CHECK at least 7 years after last contact, or until 7 years after a client turns 25 if the client was a child at the time of the service. Retention is required under the AASW Code of Ethics, the Health Records Act 2001 (Vic) CHECK, and Medicare and NDIS rules where applicable.

Records are securely destroyed at the end of the retention period.

CHECK — retention period (adults vs children), relevant Victorian legislation.

13. Data breach

Where a data breach is identified that is likely to cause serious harm, the practice will notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) under the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme. Further information is available at oaic.gov.au/privacy/notifiable-data-breaches.

14. Concerns and complaints

Concerns about the handling of personal information should be raised with the practice directly in the first instance, by phone, email, or post.

External avenues for complaint include:

  • Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC): oaic.gov.au or 1300 363 992
  • Health Complaints Commissioner Victoria: hcc.vic.gov.au or 1300 582 113
  • AASW Ethics Complaints: complaints about the practitioner's conduct as an AASW member can be directed to AASW at aasw.asn.au

15. Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. The date at the top of the page reflects the most recent update. Significant changes will be communicated to current clients directly.
